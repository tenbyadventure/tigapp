Parameter help

Mode: Recommends DCEN TIG for steels and titanium, AC TIG for aluminium, and suggests using MIX TIG on thicker aluminium if your machine has it.

Polarity: DCEN (electrode negative) for steels and titanium. AC for aluminium.

Waveform: For AC, square wave for general use and penetration, softer/sinusoidal for thin edges. N/A for DC.

AC Balance: Higher EN% = more penetration, less cleaning; lower EN% = more cleaning, less penetration.

AC Frequency: Higher Hz tightens the arc (better for thin or outside corners), lower Hz gives a softer, wider puddle (ok for thicker sections).

Pulse: Low frequency (1–2 Hz) helps control heat on thin material and vertical welds; off or low settings for thicker plate.

Gas lens: Recommended for titanium, stainless, outside corners, large cups, non-flat positions, or outdoors for more stable shielding gas.

Arc length: Try to keep the arc about 1× tungsten diameter long. Longer arcs increase heat input and instability.

Travel speed: Faster on thin material to avoid burn-through, slower on thicker material to allow penetration.

Duty cycle: Approximate safe “on-time” at a given current for a 10-minute interval.
